// about

I'm Leo, an Applied AI Data Scientist and Founding AI Engineer based on the Gold Coast.

I became interested in Machine Learning back in 2020 after watching a documentary about Cambridge Analytica, a company that used data science to help D. Trump get elected during his first term. I was stunned by what data science is capable of. I thought that if this technology could be used to manipulate public opinion, then it could be used for good as well. I signed up for a course and haven't stopped upskilling since, through self-teaching, learning from colleagues, and completing online qualifications, the most notable being the well-renowned Machine Learning Specialization by Andrew Ng and Stanford University.

That gave me a solid understanding of classical machine learning and the basic principles behind how these algorithms work. I was fortunate to learn most of this before the advent of GenAI, and before many people began self-branding as AI experts without understanding concepts like gradient descent or backpropagation, and therefore not fully grasping what these models are capable of or their limitations.

I have shipped dozens of production-ready agents from the ground up, and contributed to research and development initiatives with measurable commercial outcomes. This experience taught me many things, including the fundamentals of hypothesis testing and making decisions based on experimental outcomes rather than opinions. It also reinforced the importance of observability and the value of spending countless hours reading logs to continuously refine inputs and outputs. Using this same methodology, I have been able to repeatedly develop agents with tool access, multimodal capabilities, and persistent memory.

I prefer simple approaches and avoid unnecessary complexity. In practice, this has proven more effective than blindly following hype or adopting technologies just because they are popular. The results have consistently demonstrated that a rigorous, first-principles approach to AI engineering can deliver significant improvements in both quality and efficiency. For example, I have developed agents that compared to the ones accessible via API have had >90% cost reduction without any loss in output quality, as well as 4x their speed. So when a self-taught engineer can significantly improve AI agents built by one of the world's leading labs, it says something about the strength of my approach.
